Life Among the Ancient Egyptians

Ancient Egyptian civilization began around 3000 BC along the Nile River. The fertile lands and the annual flooding of the Nile supported agriculture and allowed society to grow.

Villages grew into larger towns and cities, with trade, industry, and a unique culture. The Egyptians created a writing system known as hieroglyphs.

Society was organized into a hierarchy led by a Pharaoh. People had diverse roles such as farming, artisanship, and craftsmanship.
